# Target Market & ICP

## Geographic focus

| Phase | Markets | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Launch (Year 1) | **Malaysia** | Two design-partner firms already; WhatsApp is dominant; English UI at v1 + BM in v1.5 (D-026); manageable regulatory surface |
| Expand (Year 2) | Singapore, Indonesia | Similar WhatsApp behavior; SG = higher willingness to pay; ID = volume |
| Reach (Year 3+) | Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, India | LINE + WhatsApp mix; price-sensitive but huge SME base |

## Vertical strategy: "Two wedges, one platform"

Launch with **construction** as wedge #1 (warm design partner) and **legal** as wedge #2 (warm design partner). The platform's template engine generalizes to any milestone-driven, client-visible workflow.

### Expansion verticals (post-PMF)

Same core motion (templated milestones + client portal + WhatsApp updates) fits:

- Interior design / architecture
- Event planning & weddings
- Migration / visa consultancies
- Accounting & tax firms (compliance calendar)
- Property management / real estate agents
- Marketing / creative agencies
- Healthcare admin (insurance claim journeys)
- Logistics & freight forwarding

Sequence by: warm intros available, template effort low, willingness to pay moderate+.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP — Construction (Malaysia, Year 1)

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| Firm type | Design-build / residential / small commercial contractor |
| Size | 5–50 employees; 1–3 project managers; 5–30 concurrent active projects |
| Project value | RM 100k – RM 5M per project |
| Client type | Homeowners, landowners, small commercial clients |
| Current tooling | Google Sheets / Excel + WhatsApp groups + Google Drive |
| Pain trigger | "Client called me angry asking for an update" / "I spent Saturday updating the sheet" |
| Decision maker | Founder/MD or Operations Director |
| Influencer | Lead project manager |
| Budget authority | Owner-led; decisions in days, not quarters |

### Primary ICP — Legal (Malaysia, Year 1)

| Dimension | Profile |
|---|---|
| Firm type | Boutique / SME law firm (5–30 lawyers) |
| Practice areas | Conveyancing, litigation, corporate, family |
| Volume | 50–500 active matters |
| Current tooling | Excel matter sheets + WhatsApp client groups + practice management system (sometimes) |
| Pain trigger | Client chases for status; partner has no fast answer |
| Decision maker | Managing partner |
| Influencer | Senior associate / practice manager |
| Budget authority | Partner-led; decisions in weeks |

### Anti-ICP (don't sell to in Year 1)

- Large general contractors > 200 staff (sold by Procore enterprise reps; long sales cycle)
- Top-tier international law firms (Clio / iManage entrenched)
- Pure freelancers (will use free Notion / Trello)
- Heavily regulated verticals (banking, pharma) — compliance burden out of scope
- Non-WhatsApp markets (US, most of EU) — channel advantage disappears

## Market sizing (Malaysia, indicative)

> Numbers are directional; refine with SSM data + MBAM/Bar Council registries during validation.

- **MY construction firms (small/medium contractors, G1–G5 CIDB categories):** ~30,000 registered; addressable subset with active project mgmt need ≈ 8,000–12,000.
- **MY law firms:** ~2,500 firms registered with the Malaysian Bar; SME segment ≈ 1,800.
- **MY interior design / architecture practices:** ~3,000 LAM/PAM-registered.
- **Combined launch SAM (MY only):** ~12,000–17,000 firms.

If we capture **2% in 3 years at ~RM 350 ARPA/month** → ~RM 1M – 1.4M MRR. Sufficient to validate; SEA expansion is the path to scale.

## Buyer journey hypotheses

1. **Trigger event** — Lost client, blown deadline, painful Saturday updating sheets, or a competitor wins a deal because they "looked more professional."
2. **Search behavior** — Google "project management software for contractors Malaysia", asks in WhatsApp/Telegram industry groups, sees competitor's branded client link.
3. **Evaluation** — Wants a **demo with their own template** in < 30 mins. Will not read whitepapers. Trial > sales call.
4. **Buying** — Signs up on a credit card or local payment (FPX/GrabPay). Owner decides; no procurement.
5. **Activation** — First success = first client receives a WhatsApp update from the firm's branded sender. That's the "aha".
6. **Expansion** — Adds more projects, more team members, then more notification channels.

## Channels to reach the ICP

| Channel | Why it works for this ICP | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Industry associations** (MBAM, REHDA, PAM, Malaysian Bar) | Trust, list rentals, event sponsorships | Medium |
| **Founder-led sales** (LinkedIn + warm intros from design partners) | Owners trust other owners; short cycles | Low / time |
| **Local accountants & consultants** (referral partners) | They see firms' pain; recommend tools | Low + rev share |
| **WhatsApp & FB groups** (contractor groups, lawyer groups) | Where the ICP already lives | Very low |
| **Case studies + branded client links** | Every client who receives a Siapp update sees the brand | Compounding / zero marginal |
| **MDEC / SME Corp grants** | Subsidizes adoption; co-marketing | Medium time, high yield |
| **Google search (long-tail)** | "free contractor project management Malaysia" intent | Medium |
