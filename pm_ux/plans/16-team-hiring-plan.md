# Team & Hiring Plan

People are the most expensive and most slow-moving lever. Hire late, hire deliberately, and hire for the next 12 months — not the future org chart.

## Founding team — clear roles

Even with two founders, write down who owns what. Ambiguity will kill speed.

| Role | Owner | Decision authority |
|---|---|---|
| CEO / Commercial | Founder A | Pricing, sales, partnerships, funding |
| CTO / Product | Founder B | Architecture, hiring engineers, roadmap |
| Both jointly | Mission/vision, key hires, ToS-grade legal, fundraising |

Operating agreements:
- **One-day disagreement rule:** if not resolved in 24h, the role-owner decides; the other records dissent in writing.
- **Pre-commitment to founder vesting** (4 yr, 1 yr cliff) before either has anything to lose.
- **Quarterly founder retro** — relationship maintenance is work.

## Hiring sequence (by phase)

### Phase 0 (Months 0–3) — founders + contractors

| Role | Type | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Founders × 2 | FT | Discovery, design, partner work |
| Contract designer | Project | Brand, key UI screens |
| Contract counsel | Project | Entity, contracts, PDPA |
| Bookkeeper | Outsourced | Stay clean from day 1 |

### Phase 1 (Months 3–9) — first 2 hires

| # | Role | When | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Founding engineer** (full-stack TS) | Month 3 | We need to build; CTO shouldn't be only coder |
| 2 | **Customer success / onboarding** (BM preferred, not required at v1 — D-026) | Month 5–6 | Time-to-first-WhatsApp matters; CS prevents churn |

Notes:
- Equity for #1 should be **meaningful** (1.5–3% range, validate with MY market data).
- #2 can start part-time and convert.

### Phase 2 (Months 9–18) — scale the proven motion

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| **Second engineer** (full-stack) | Capacity for integrations + reliability work |
| **Designer** (product) | UI debt grows; client portal evolves |
| **Sales lead** (relationships in MY construction or legal) | Founder transitions to leadership; cycles repeat |
| **Partnerships / channel manager** | Run association partnerships + grants |
| **Content marketer** (BM-capable for v1.5 BM launch) | Owns SEO, case studies, BM editorial |

### Phase 3 (Months 18–36) — regional + depth

- Engineers (2–4)
- Country manager — Indonesia (relocate or local hire)
- Country manager — Singapore (part-time first)
- Customer success leads per region
- DevOps / SRE (when reliability load justifies)
- Finance lead (when ARR > USD 1M)
- People ops (when team > 20)

## Roles we will not hire early (and why)

- **CFO** — outsourced fractional through Phase 2.
- **Head of Marketing** — founders own marketing until repeatable channels emerge.
- **VP Engineering** — premature with < 5 engineers.
- **Product Manager** — founders are PMs in Phase 1; first dedicated PM around Month 18.
- **Recruiter** — founder + founding engineer recruit until ≥ 3 hires/quarter.

## Compensation philosophy

- **Below-market cash, fair equity, generous transparency.** No "competitive comp" lies.
- Public salary bands per level (internally); explain trade-off to candidates.
- **Equity refresh** annually for keepers; vest cliffs honored.
- **Bonuses tied to company-level outcomes**, not individual stretch goals (avoids gaming).
- **Remote-friendly** with quarterly in-person weeks; bias hiring within MY/SG timezone (UTC+8 ± 2).

## Hiring filters (deliberate)

For every hire, the candidate must clear:

1. **Mission fit** — do they care about client trust in SEA professional services?
2. **Pace** — they've shipped weekly in a < 20-person environment.
3. **Local intuition** — they have lived/worked in a region we're selling to.
4. **Range** — they will do work below their title (especially first 5 hires).
5. **Boring competence** — they will not break tests, secrets, or production at midnight.

## Onboarding (first 30 days for any hire)

- Day 1: laptop, accounts, 1:1 with each founder, a real customer interview to listen to.
- Week 1: ship something small to production (or a non-engineering equivalent).
- Week 2: pair with CS on 3 customer conversations.
- Week 3: lead a small feature or workstream end-to-end.
- Day 30: written reflection, 1:1 with CEO; mutual yes-go for permanent.

## Team operating norms

- **Calendar:** focus mornings, meetings afternoons (MY time).
- **Async-default communication**: decisions written down, not relived in standup.
- **Slack channels** mirror our domain: `#firm-app`, `#client-portal`, `#messaging`, `#customers`, `#deals`, `#oncall`.
- **No work on Sundays.** Sunday email predicts attrition.

## Advisors

Recruit 2–3 with **specific** value. Examples:

1. A senior leader from MY construction (e.g. ex-GM of a mid-sized contractor) — operator credibility.
2. A senior partner from an MY law firm — legal vertical credibility.
3. A SaaS founder who has scaled in SEA — go-to-market patterns.

Compensation: small equity (0.25–0.5%), 1-year cliff, defined deliverables (quarterly review, intros, occasional public co-sign).

## Hiring red flags

- Loves the idea, hasn't asked about customers.
- Talks more than they ask in the interview.
- Has never shipped to production in a small team.
- Negotiates aggressively on title before joining.
- Cannot articulate a recent mistake honestly.

## What we measure on people

- **Time-to-first-impact:** for engineers, days to first production commit; for CS, days to own first 5 accounts.
- **Retention:** voluntary attrition > 15% annual = systemic problem.
- **Internal NPS** quarterly with 1 question: "Would you recommend joining Siapp to a peer?"
- **Hiring funnel:** offers / interviews / sourced. Watch interview-to-offer; > 6:1 means our bar or our pitch is off.
